Introduction

Significance of the Study

This study aims to benefit the Badjao community, our partner institution. The value of composting to the community is essential, due to the Badjao communityâ€™s issues with their lack of basic needs, such as food. Measuring the speed of the composting process in natural fertilizers can help them with speeding up the growth process of their homegrown crops. Researching this is also important to the environment because it can help decrease pollution and help grow plants, as instead of throwing away compostables, we can reutilize them as fertilizer and help make plants grow.

Problem Statement and Hypothesis

Which type of natural fertilizer, between banana peels and eggshells, is more effective on a tomato plant?

If banana peels were used as fertilizer, then it would be more effective than eggshells.

Methodology

Variables

Independent Variable
-Type of natural fertilizer

Dependent Variable
-Size of leaves

Controlled Variables
-Kind of soil
-Size of pot
-Level of moisture

Set-ups

Control Set-up
No fertilizer, 3 pots of soil, 3 tomato plant seedlings, 500 ml of water for each pot every 3 days, indirect sunlight, 15 days duration.

Experimental Set-up 1
Banana peels fertilizer, 3 pots of soil, 3 tomato plant seedlings, 500 ml of water for each pot every 3 days, indirect sunlight, 15 days duration

Experimental Set-up 2
Eggshells fertilizer, 3 pots of soil, 3 tomato plant seedlings, 500 ml of water for each pot every 3 days, indirect sunlight, 15 days duration

Procedure

Eggshell Fertilizer
- Collect the Eggshells
- Wash the eggshells to remove the bacteria
- Boil the eggshells in water for 5 minutes, dry in sunlight and crush
the eggshells after
- Sprinkle the fine eggshell power to the soil before putting the
tomato seed
- When planting the tomatoes, dig at least 2 inch into the soil

Banana Peel Fertilizer
- Collect banana peels
- Cut banana peels into small pieces
- Place banana peels in a container then fill it with water
- Wait for a couple of days then your fertilizer is ready to be
used

Pot Preparation
- Prepare your 9 (8 inch size) pots
- Label the pots with a marker
- Label the controlled set-up as C1, C2, C3,
- Label the Experimental set-up 1 as A1, A2, A3
- Label the Experimental set-up as B1, B2, B3
