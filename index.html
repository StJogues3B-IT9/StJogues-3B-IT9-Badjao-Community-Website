Summary/Introduction

Unhygienic food and water access is a serious issue in the badjao community. These issues have been recognized as serious dangers to poor quality of life and human health. Due to the bad sanitation in the Badjao community, access to clean food and water has become a major problem. All of the people of the Badjao community experience health issues like diarrhea, starvation, and even death as a result of the unclean environment. Access to clean food and water are important because people can live well and have a decent quality of life if they have access to these. The benefit of having access to healthy food and water is that it will enhance their quality of life and create a happier, healthier community. By bringing communities together to help bring about change on issues, it raises and spreads awareness. People can feel encouraged and motivated to act or make changes when they are aware.

Profile

Pastor Felicisimo "Jun" Morales established Matina Aplaya Inc.'s Badjao Community Association. He and his wife spent more than 8 years living among the Badjao on Isla Verde, and he founded the group with the intention of "becoming a model among the Badjao community's inhabitants." From roughly 35 families when it first began, there are currently more than 700 people there, divided among 174 households. With its activities and services, the institution succeeds in raising the level of living in the Badjao neighborhood. Felicisimo Morales, the incorporation's creator, established it after assisting a needy beggar and asked if there was anything further he could do. He and his wife Daisy made the decision to go out to tackle the difficulties presented by the Badjaos and discover solutions to improve their standard of living. They must discover how to manage their needs for food, water, clothing, cleanliness, and much more, but they manage. Morales' perseverance allowed him to improve other people's lives. The ADDU JHS Community is one of the beneficiaries of the Badjao community. In the past, Ateneo donated trash segregation supplies to the Badjao neighborhood in an effort to keep the area clean. And this year, we are assisting the Badjao community in finding answers to their problems with clean water and food.

Context

Mission

In order to improve people's lives, encourage sustainable development, and promote good leadership in the nation, the Badjao community group collaborates with the government and industry. The goal of the badjao community's advocacy is to raise everyone's standard of living. Our mission is to promote community issues' awareness, comprehension, and education.Lastly it is to spread awareness and understanding in order to develop and maintain a healthy environment.

History

The Badjao Community Association experience discrimination and self-fulfilling prophecy. They were tagged as slow learners, have poor hygiene, and have low emotional quotients. Because of a language barrier, they have difficulty interacting with other people and expressing their ideas and needs.

Organizer

The Badjao Community Association of Matina Aplaya Inc. was founded by Pastor Felicisimo “Jun” Morales. With his wife, he lived with the Badjao in Isla Verde for more than 8 years, and with the purpose of “becoming a model among the populace of the badjao community”, he established the association. It started with about 35 families, but now they have grown to more than 700 members from 174 families. The institution aims to raise the Badjao community’s standard of living, and it does just that with their programs and services.

Beneficiaries

The founder of the incorporation, Felicisimo Morales, created it after helping a poor beggar and asking if he could do more. He and his wife, Daisy, decided to go off and face the challenges of the Badjaos and find ways to make their lives more comfortable. They have to figure out how to manage food, water, clothes, hygiene and so much more and yet, they are still able to manage. Because of Morales' determination, he managed to make some people's lives better.

Benefactors

One of the benefactors of the Badjao community is the ADDU JHS Community. In the past, Ateneo provided the Badjao community with waste segregation materials in order to preserve the cleanliness of their environment. And now, this year, we are helping the Badjao community in their issues of clean water and food by researching solutions to this problem.
